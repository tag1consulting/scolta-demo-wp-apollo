# Sources and Attributions

## Historical Sources

All Apollo program facts, dates, mission statistics, and biographical details were drawn from the historical record. Primary references include:

- NASA mission reports and press kits (public domain)
- NASA History Division — history.nasa.gov
- Air and Space Museum collections — airandspace.si.edu
- "Carrying the Fire" by Michael Collins (1974)
- "First Man: The Life of Neil A. Armstrong" by James Hansen (2005)
- "Failure Is Not an Option" by Gene Kranz (2000)
- "Apollo: The Race to the Moon" by Charles Murray & Catherine Bly Cox (1989)
- NASA SP-4009: "The Apollo Spacecraft: A Chronology"
- NASA TM-2009-214800: Apollo by the Numbers
- NASA Air-to-Ground Voice Transcripts (public domain)

## Images

NASA images are in the public domain. No images have been committed to this repository as of initial creation. Recommended sources for future image additions:

- **NASA Image and Video Library**: images.nasa.gov
  - Search by mission: "Apollo 11", "Apollo 15 Rover", "Earthrise", etc.
  - All NASA imagery is public domain unless otherwise noted
- **NASA JSC Digital Image Collection**: archive.org/details/nasajsc
- **The Planetary Society image archive**

When adding images, record the NASA image ID, collection, and original photographer/source in this file.

## Narrator Voice

The blog posts are written from the perspective of a fictional anonymous American civilian narrator (living near Columbus, Ohio) watching the Space Age unfold from 1957–1973. All opinions, reactions, and interior observations are fictional. Historical facts referenced within the posts are accurate to the best research available.

Characters referenced:
- **The narrator**: unnamed, b. ~1925, insurance adjuster, Ohio
- **Betty**: the narrator's wife
- **Harold**: neighbor, Korean War veteran
- **Edna**: Harold's wife
